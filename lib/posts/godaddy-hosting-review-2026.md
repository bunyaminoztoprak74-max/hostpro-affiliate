**Bottom line:** GoDaddy hosting is not where we'd recommend most users spend their money in 2026. Performance trails competitors, support quality is inconsistent, and upsell pressure is aggressive. Better options exist at every price point — [Hostinger](/blog/hostinger-review-2026) for budget, [SiteGround](/blog/siteground-review-2026) for reliability.

## The Upsell Problem

GoDaddy's checkout process is notorious for aggressive upselling. During our test purchase, the default cart included:

- Domain privacy (normally free on competitors): +$9.99/year
- SSL upgrade (free SSL is sufficient): +$79.99/year
- Website backup: +$2.99/month
- Email marketing suite: +$14.99/month
- Security scan: +$5.99/month

**Total unadded cost if you don't manually deselect:** ~$155/year in extras you didn't ask for. Read your cart carefully.

## Support Quality

GoDaddy offers 24/7 phone support — a genuine differentiator. But quality is inconsistent:
- **Phone:** Available immediately, but first-line agents often upsell before troubleshooting
- **Live chat:** Available 24/7, quality mixed
- **Knowledge base:** Large but poorly organized — search often returns outdated articles

In 8 support tests, 3 calls began with an agent recommending an upgrade before addressing the actual issue. Escalation to technical support was more helpful.

## When GoDaddy Makes Sense

Despite our criticisms, GoDaddy works well for:
1. **Domain + hosting convenience:** If you want everything with one provider and don't need top performance
2. **Website builder users:** GoDaddy's website builder is intuitive for absolute beginners
3. **Business phone + email:** GoDaddy's Microsoft 365 bundles are competitively priced
4. **Brand recognition:** Some clients or employers specifically request "GoDaddy" — in which case it's fine

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- 24/7 phone support
- Largest hosting provider — widely recognized
- One-stop-shop for domains, email, hosting
- Managed WordPress has acceptable performance
- Easy website builder for non-technical users

**Cons:**
- Shared hosting TTFB (380–412ms) is poor

- Renewal rates are 3× promotional prices
- Shared uptime (99.81%) below industry average
- Support quality inconsistent with frequent upsell pressure

## Final Verdict

GoDaddy earns **3.7/5** — the lowest score among the mainstream hosts we've reviewed. It's not terrible (managed WordPress is functional), but it's consistently outperformed by competitors at every price point. The upselling practices are frustrating and reduce trust. If you're starting fresh in 2026, [Hostinger](/blog/hostinger-review-2026) at $3.99/month or [SiteGround](/blog/siteground-review-2026) at $7.99/month are better choices by nearly every measure.

Choose GoDaddy if you have existing domains there and value the convenience of one provider. Otherwise, look elsewhere.
