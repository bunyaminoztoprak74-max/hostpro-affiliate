### Image Optimization for Photography Websites

Photography websites need aggressive image optimization — high-res images are the enemy of fast loading:

**Before uploading:**
1. Resize to web-appropriate dimensions (2000px wide max for full-width)
2. Export as WebP (30-50% smaller than JPEG at equivalent quality)
3. Target 200-500KB per image (not the 5MB+ camera exports)

**WordPress plugins for photographers:**
- **Smush (free):** Automatic optimization, lazy loading, WebP conversion
- **ShortPixel ($7/month):** Best compression quality, WebP support
- **Imagify:** Good alternative with similar features

**CDN for photographers:**
- Enable host's included CDN first (free)
- Cloudinary free tier (25GB, 25GB bandwidth) for additional image hosting
- Cloudflare CDN as DNS-level CDN (free, excellent)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should photographers use Squarespace or WordPress for their portfolio?**
WordPress gives more control, customization, and SEO potential. Squarespace's built-in templates look beautiful but limit flexibility. For photographers who want to rank on Google for "[city] wedding photographer" or similar searches, WordPress's SEO flexibility gives a significant advantage.

**How do I deliver client proofing galleries online?**
Options: (1) NextGEN Gallery Pro on WordPress with password-protected galleries. (2) Dedicated services like Pic-Time, Gallery, or ShootProof (may be more cost-effective for high-volume). (3) Cloud storage links (Dropbox, Google Drive) for simple delivery. WordPress gallery plugins work well for most photographers.

**Can I sell prints directly from my photography website?**
Yes. WooCommerce (free) + a print fulfillment integration (WHCC, Bay Photo, or Printful) allows direct print sales from your WordPress website. Hostinger Business and SiteGround both support WooCommerce well.

**How much bandwidth do client galleries use?**
Client gallery bandwidth depends on: number of clients, images per gallery, and how many full-res downloads they make. For a typical wedding photographer delivering 500 images/wedding and 20 weddings/year: ~50GB bandwidth/year, well within any major host's "unlimited" threshold.

**Is it better to host gallery images on my website or use a third-party service?**
Hosting on your website (with CDN) is cost-effective and keeps everything under your control. Third-party services (Shootproof, Pic-Time) offer client communication tools and print sales integration that may be worth the additional cost. Many photographers use both: WordPress for their public portfolio and a dedicated service for client galleries.
