## Rating Methodology

We score each host across 5 weighted categories:

| Category | Weight | What We Measure |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| **Performance** | 30% | TTFB, page load time, Core Web Vitals |
| **Reliability** | 25% | Annual uptime percentage, incident count |
| **Features** | 20% | Control panel, staging, backups, email, SSL |
| **Support** | 15% | Response time, resolution quality, availability |
| **Value** | 10% | Price vs performance, renewal rates, money-back |

Each category is scored 1-10 based on comparative data within each tier. A budget host is compared to budget competitors; managed hosts are compared to other managed hosts.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Are higher-rated hosts worth the premium price?**
In most cases, yes. The performance difference between a 9.0+ rated host and a 6.0 rated host is significant — 2x faster TTFB, 9x less annual downtime, dramatically better support. The price difference is often just $2-5/month.

**Should I trust a hosting provider with a 4.9-star rating everywhere?**
Be skeptical of perfect or near-perfect ratings. Every hosting provider has weaknesses. Sites giving every host 4.8-5.0 stars are either not testing rigorously or are prioritizing commission income over honest ratings.

**How do renewal prices affect value ratings?**
Significantly. A host with a $2.95/month intro price that renews at $10.99/month has worse long-term value than a host at $3.99/month that renews at $8.99/month — even though the intro price looks better. We factor in 3-year total cost in our value ratings.

**What's the most important rating category?**
Reliability (uptime) matters most for business sites. Performance (speed) matters most for SEO and user experience. Support matters most for users who aren't technically confident. Value matters most when budget is constrained.

**Can ratings change year over year?**
Yes. Hosts improve (SiteGround rebuilt their infrastructure in 2020 and dramatically improved ratings) or decline (HostGator has declined as EIG/Newfold hasn't invested in infrastructure). Ratings from 2021-2022 are likely outdated.
