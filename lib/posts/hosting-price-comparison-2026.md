## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why is the introductory hosting price so much lower than renewal?**
Customer acquisition is expensive in the hosting industry. Introductory rates reduce the barrier to signup. Hosting companies know that most customers stay even when renewal rates are higher — the switching cost (migration time, DNS changes) creates inertia. Budget for the renewal rate from day one.

**Is the cheapest hosting always the worst?**
No. Namecheap at $1.98/month is genuinely cheap with mediocre performance. But Hostinger at $2.99/month is cheap AND fast. Price is not a reliable indicator of performance — server technology matters more than price.

**Are there any hosting providers with truly transparent pricing?**
WP Engine, Kinsta, and Cloudways don't play the intro/renewal game — the price you sign up at is the price you'll always pay. This is rare in the industry and represents genuine pricing integrity.

**Should I lock in a 2 or 3 year plan for the lowest rate?**
Only if you're confident in the provider after testing during the money-back period. A 3-year commitment at a lower rate is attractive, but migration mid-term is still possible. Hostinger's 4-year plan offers the deepest discount, but verify performance in the first 30 days before committing long-term.

**What's the real cost difference between Hostinger and SiteGround over 5 years?**
Hostinger Premium 5-year estimate: ~$495. SiteGround GrowBig 5-year estimate: ~$767. SiteGround costs ~55% more over 5 years. Whether the performance advantage (148ms vs 168ms TTFB) justifies $272 extra over 5 years depends on your site's revenue and traffic.
