## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can nonprofits get free web hosting?**
Some options exist: DreamHost's nonprofit donation program, WordPress.com's nonprofit plan (limited features), and The Giving Block (crypto donation platform) includes free hosting for qualifying orgs. Generally, the application process for free hosting takes time that many nonprofits don't have. At $3-4/month, paid hosting is often worth the simplicity.

**What hosting is best for a nonprofit that needs to run fundraising campaigns?**
SiteGround or Hostinger. Both provide reliable uptime and fast performance during campaign launches when traffic spikes. Pair with Cloudflare CDN (free) to handle traffic bursts during major fundraising drives.

**How do nonprofits handle data privacy with donor information?**
Your hosting provider must be compliant with relevant data privacy laws (GDPR for European donors, state laws in the US). Keep donor data in your CRM (CiviCRM, Salesforce Nonprofit, or GiveWP donor management) rather than exposing it in your website database. Use SSL (included with all recommended hosts) to encrypt all data transmission.

**Can volunteers with no tech skills manage the nonprofit website?**
Yes, with the right setup. WordPress with Gutenberg block editor is manageable for non-technical users. Create user accounts with "Editor" role (can manage content, not server settings). Use a simple, stable theme. Avoid complex page builders that require technical knowledge to troubleshoot.

**What's the best free email option for nonprofits?**
Google Workspace for Nonprofits provides Gmail with your domain for free (up to 100 accounts) for qualifying organizations. Apply through Google for Nonprofits. This is the best nonprofit email solution and pairs with any hosting provider.
