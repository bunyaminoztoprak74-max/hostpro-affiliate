NordPass and LastPass both generate, store and autofill credentials across common devices. The deciding issue is not a single feature checkbox: it is how much weight you give product history, recovery design, account administration and your own migration risk.

## Quick Verdict

Choose **NordPass** for a clean interface, modern encryption design and a fresh start away from LastPass's disclosed security incidents. Existing LastPass teams may value its mature administration and integrations, but should review incident disclosures and current controls before renewing.

| Factor | NordPass | LastPass |
|---|---|---|
| Best for | Individuals and simple teams | Established deployments |
| Free tier | Available | Available with limitations |
| Passkeys | Supported | Supported |
| Main concern | Younger ecosystem | Historical security incidents |

## Security Model

Both products use a zero-knowledge design intended to keep vault contents unreadable to the provider. That does not eliminate risk. Weak master passwords, malicious browser extensions, stolen sessions and exposed recovery methods can bypass otherwise strong encryption. Use a unique, long master passphrase and phishing-resistant MFA.

## Migration Safety

Exports are commonly unencrypted CSV files. Complete the move on a trusted, patched computer; import immediately; verify a sample of records; then securely remove the export and empty synced recycle bins. Rotate the most sensitive credentials after migration.
