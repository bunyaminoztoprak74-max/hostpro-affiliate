Shared hosting powers the majority of websites on the internet — from personal blogs to established business sites. The quality gap between providers has widened significantly, making host selection more important than ever.

We ran 9 shared hosting providers through 6 months of continuous testing in 2026. Here's what the data shows.

## Testing Methodology

Each host was tested with an identical WordPress site (Astra theme, 12 plugins, 50 posts with images). We measured:

- **TTFB** (Time to First Byte) — 2,160 measurements per host over 6 months
- **Uptime** — 1-minute interval monitoring from 5 global locations
- **Support quality** — 3 tickets per host, measured response time and first-contact resolution
- **WordPress admin speed** — page load time inside wp-admin

## Who Shared Hosting Is For

**Shared hosting is the right choice when:**
- Your site gets under 100,000 monthly visitors
- You're launching a new site or business
- You want to minimize hosting costs in early stages
- You're running a WordPress blog, portfolio, or small business site

**Upgrade to VPS or managed WordPress when:**
- Traffic consistently exceeds 100K/month with slow page loads
- You need custom server software or specific PHP extensions
- Your site runs resource-intensive applications (complex WooCommerce, membership sites)

For most websites — even established ones with good traffic — quality shared hosting from Hostinger or SiteGround is entirely sufficient.
