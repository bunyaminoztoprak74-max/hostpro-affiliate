## What Actually Matters for Small Business Websites

### 1. Uptime Above Everything

A website that's down is a business that can't make sales. Calculate what your downtime tolerance is:

| Monthly Traffic | Revenue/Month | Cost of 1-Hour Downtime |
|----------------|--------------|------------------------|
| 1,000 visitors | $500 | $25 |
| 5,000 visitors | $2,500 | $125 |
| 20,000 visitors | $10,000 | $500 |

Higher traffic = more expensive downtime = more justification for premium hosting.

### 2. Professional Email Is Non-Negotiable

Sending business emails from gmail.com or hotmail.com instantly undermines your professional credibility. Every host in our top recommendations includes professional email hosting. Use it.

### 3. Daily Backups Are Insurance

What happens when you install a plugin that breaks your site at 10pm? Without daily backups, you're manually rebuilding. With daily backups, you restore to yesterday and it takes 2 minutes. Daily backups are non-negotiable for business websites.

### 4. SSL and Security

Google Chrome now marks non-HTTPS sites as "Not Secure" — killing trust and conversions. All our recommended hosts include free SSL. Additionally, look for:
- Web Application Firewall (WAF)
- DDoS protection
- Malware scanning

SiteGround and WP Engine include all three. Hostinger includes SSL and basic security.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should a small business use shared or cloud hosting?**
Most small businesses start with shared hosting (SiteGround, Hostinger) and upgrade to cloud (Cloudways) or managed (WP Engine) when traffic or revenue justify the cost. There's no reason to pay for cloud hosting until you actually need the scalability.

**Does hosting affect my business's Google ranking?**
Yes. Page speed (influenced by hosting) is a Google ranking factor via Core Web Vitals. A faster host gives you a technical SEO advantage. Beyond speed, uptime matters — a site that's frequently down loses crawlability and index freshness.

**What's the most important hosting feature for a WooCommerce store?**
Uptime first, then server-level caching for product pages, then daily backups. WooCommerce sites also benefit from hosts that specifically optimize for WooCommerce (SiteGround, WP Engine, Kinsta all do this).

**Is website speed really that important for small businesses?**
Absolutely. Studies consistently show that page load time under 2 seconds maximizes conversions. A host like Hostinger (168ms TTFB) gives your pages a head start vs. a slow host (400ms TTFB). This matters especially on mobile, where networks are slower.

**How do I migrate my existing small business website to a better host?**
Most hosts offer free migration assistance. SiteGround, WP Engine, and Hostinger all provide free WordPress migration. The process typically takes 1-4 hours and can be done without downtime if you plan the DNS change carefully.
