HostGator was a budget hosting staple for years. Acquired by EIG (now Newfold Digital) in 2012, it's shared infrastructure and ownership with Bluehost, iPage, and dozens of other brands. That consolidation has consequences — good and bad.

**Bottom line:** HostGator is adequate but not competitive in 2026. Shared hosting TTFB (389ms) is among the slowest we've tested. [Hostinger](/blog/hostinger-review-2026) offers better performance at a lower price. [SiteGround](/blog/siteground-review-2026) offers better performance for serious WordPress sites at similar renewal pricing. HostGator is a reasonable fallback if you have existing accounts, but we wouldn't recommend starting there.

## The Newfold Infrastructure Issue

HostGator shares server infrastructure with Bluehost, iPage, and other Newfold brands. This means:

- **Shared security incidents:** A compromise affecting Newfold infrastructure can affect multiple brands simultaneously
- **Shared congestion:** Overselling on shared infrastructure contributes to slow TTFB
- **Unified support:** Support teams handle multiple brands — specialization is reduced

This isn't unique to HostGator (many budget hosts consolidate), but it's worth understanding when comparing "independent" options like A2 Hosting or Namecheap.

## Where HostGator Still Works

1. **Legacy sites:** If you have an existing HostGator setup that works, migrating isn't always worth the effort
2. **Unmetered storage:** "Unlimited" storage on Baby plans is genuinely useful for sites with lots of media
3. **45-day money-back guarantee:** Longer than the industry standard 30 days
4. **Phone support:** 24/7 phone access is useful for non-technical users
5. **cPanel familiarity:** Full cPanel access on all shared plans

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**

- Full cPanel access

**Cons:**
- Shared TTFB (389ms) is among the slowest in our tests
- Uptime (99.86%) fell below their SLA guarantee in our test
- Newfold/EIG consolidated infrastructure (same as Bluehost)
- Aggressive checkout upselling
- Renewal rates are 3× promotional prices
- QuickStart WP managed WordPress still slower than Hostinger basic

## Final Verdict

HostGator earns **3.8/5** — slightly better than GoDaddy due to its 45-day guarantee and consistent (if slow) infrastructure, but not competitive against modern budget alternatives. In 2026, [Hostinger](/blog/hostinger-review-2026) is the better budget choice in every measurable way: faster TTFB, lower renewal rates, NVMe storage, and comparable 24/7 support. [SiteGround](/blog/siteground-review-2026) is the better choice for serious WordPress performance at the $7–15/month renewal range.

We can't recommend starting a new site on HostGator when better alternatives exist at the same price.
