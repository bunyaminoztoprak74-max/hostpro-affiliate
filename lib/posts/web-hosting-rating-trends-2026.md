## Rating Framework: How We Score

Our rating system evaluates five weighted categories, each based on measurable criteria:

- **Performance (30%):** TTFB, load time, Core Web Vitals results
- **Reliability (25%):** Annual uptime percentage, incident frequency and severity
- **Features (20%):** Control panel quality, backups, staging, CDN, email
- **Support (15%):** Response time, resolution rate, WordPress knowledge depth
- **Value (10%):** Price vs performance, renewal rate transparency, included features

## How Top-Rated Hosts Earn Their Ratings

### What Separates 9.0+ Hosts from Average Hosts

**Infrastructure investment:**
Top-rated hosts have made deliberate technology choices: NVMe SSD storage, LiteSpeed or Nginx web servers, and proprietary caching systems. These aren't default configurations — they require ongoing investment.

**Operational expertise:**
SiteGround's 99.99% uptime doesn't happen by accident. It requires redundant architecture, proactive monitoring, and a skilled operations team that responds 24/7 to infrastructure events.

**WordPress-specific knowledge:**
WP Engine and Kinsta don't just run WordPress — they understand it deeply. Their support teams can discuss PHP opcache, WordPress hooks, database optimization, and WooCommerce caching nuances that most general-purpose hosting support teams can't.

**Honest pricing:**
Top-rated hosts are more transparent about costs. WP Engine and Kinsta have no intro/renewal price gap. Hostinger's renewal rates are moderate. The worst-rated hosts (GoDaddy) combine aggressive upselling with the highest effective costs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I always choose the highest-rated host?**
Choose the highest-rated host for your specific category. WP Engine earns a 9.4/10 overall, but it's designed for managed WordPress — using it for a personal blog would be overpaying for features you don't need. Hostinger at 9.1/10 is better for personal projects.

**How do customer review platforms differ from expert ratings?**
Platforms like Trustpilot or G2 aggregate user opinions. These trend negative (unhappy customers leave reviews; satisfied ones don't). Expert ratings like ours measure objective metrics regardless of volume of opinions. Neither is purely "better" — they measure different things.

**Do high-rated hosts ever decline in quality?**
Yes. HostGator was well-regarded in 2013-2016 but has declined consistently as infrastructure investment lagged behind competitors. Check the date of any rating — hosting quality changes significantly over 3-5 years.

**Can a low-rated host be right for some users?**
In rare cases. Namecheap (7.3/10) is the cheapest long-term option and adequate for low-traffic personal sites where budget is the overriding priority. Their low rating reflects below-average speed and features — which may be acceptable tradeoffs for users with specific constraints.

**What's the best way to verify a hosting rating before buying?**
Sign up during the money-back guarantee window, install your WordPress site, run GTmetrix to verify TTFB, set up UptimeRobot to monitor uptime, and submit a technical support ticket. 30 days is enough to verify the most critical rating factors.

## Year-over-Year Rating Changes: Who Improved and Who Declined

The hosting landscape shifts significantly from year to year. Here's how each provider's rating changed from 2024 to 2026, and the specific infrastructure or policy decisions that drove the change:

| Host | 2024 Rating | 2026 Rating | Change | What Changed |
|------|------------|------------|--------|-------------|
| **Hostinger** | 8.5/10 | 9.1/10 | +0.6 | NVMe SSD rolled out to ALL plans (was Turbo-only), LiteSpeed Cache improvements, hPanel redesign |
| **Kinsta** | 9.0/10 | 9.3/10 | +0.3 | Google Cloud C2 upgrade, new APM tool, Cloudflare CDN integration (from Fastly) |

**The pattern:** Hosts that invested in NVMe SSD storage and modern web server software improved significantly. The NVMe rollout at Hostinger is particularly noteworthy — moving every plan from SATA SSD to NVMe SSD delivered measurable performance improvements at no additional cost to users.

### Hosts That DECLINED (2024→2026)

| Host | 2024 Rating | 2026 Rating | Change | What Changed (for the worse) |
|------|------------|------------|--------|------------------------------|
| **Bluehost** | 7.9/10 | 7.4/10 | -0.5 | Newfold Digital consolidation = shared infrastructure, slower TTFB (312ms up from 289ms), support quality declined |
