## Security Model

Both use zero-knowledge designs intended to prevent the provider from reading vault contents. Proton states that all vault fields, including usernames, web addresses and notes, receive end-to-end encryption and publishes its apps as open source. Product architecture matters, but account security still depends on a strong master passphrase, MFA, protected recovery methods and clean devices.

## Proton Pass Advantages

Proton Pass combines credential management with hide-my-email aliases. The free plan currently lists unlimited logins, unlimited devices, passkeys and a limited number of aliases. Paid tiers add unlimited aliases, integrated 2FA, secure sharing, dark-web monitoring, emergency access and custom-domain support.

That makes Proton especially attractive when alias-based privacy is part of the workflow, or when Proton Mail, Drive and VPN are already in use.

## NordPass Advantages

NordPass is easier to evaluate as a focused password manager without committing to a broader privacy suite. Its interface and migration path suit users moving away from browser storage or LastPass. ## Migration and Recovery

Password-manager exports are often unencrypted CSV files. Export only on a trusted device, import immediately, verify representative records and securely delete the file from local and synced recycle bins. Rotate the highest-value credentials after migration and store recovery codes offline.

Before paying, test autofill, passkeys, recovery and export on every operating system you depend on. A vault that works perfectly on one browser but poorly on a work phone is not the right choice.
