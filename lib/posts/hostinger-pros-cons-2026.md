## Hostinger Cons: Where They Fall Short

### Con 1: Weekly Backups on Lower Plans ⭐⭐

**The problem:** Single and Premium plans include weekly automated backups. For a blog that publishes daily, a full week of content could be lost if the backup is from 6 days ago.

**Mitigation:** Upgrade to Business plan ($5.99/month) for daily backups, or install UpdraftPlus (free) to backup daily to Dropbox or Google Drive.

**Impact level:** Medium — significant for business sites, less significant for occasional bloggers

**Score:** 6/10

---

### Con 2: No Phone Support ⭐⭐

**The problem:** Hostinger offers 24/7 live chat only — no phone support. For users who prefer speaking to someone, especially during urgent issues, this is a limitation.

**Mitigation:** The live chat is fast (2-minute response) and effective. For most technical issues, chat is more efficient than phone anyway.

**Impact level:** Low-Medium — significant for phone-preferring users, minimal for chat-comfortable users

**Score:** 6.5/10

---

### Con 3: Renewal Price Increase ⭐⭐⭐

**The numbers:**
- Premium intro: $3.99/mo → Renewal: $8.99/mo (+125%)
- Business intro: $5.99/mo → Renewal: $14.99/mo (+150%)

**Context:** This is industry standard, but it's still a real cost increase. Factor the renewal price into your budget from the start.

**Mitigation:** Hostinger's renewal rates are still lower than Bluehost ($10.99/mo) and SiteGround ($14.99/mo). Even at renewal, Hostinger provides good value relative to performance.

**Impact level:** Medium — financial planning required from day one

**Score:** 6.5/10

---

### Con 4: Single Plan Is Too Restrictive ⭐⭐

**The problem:** The $2.99/mo Single plan limits you to 1 website. You can't even have a staging site and live site simultaneously. Most users quickly discover they need the Premium plan.

**Reality:** The Single plan is effectively a marketing tool to show a low entry price. Most users need Premium ($3.99/mo) from day one.

**Impact level:** Low — just choose Premium from the start

**Score:** 7/10 (once you account for real pricing)

---

### Con 5: Limited AI Features ⭐⭐⭐

**The marketing:** Hostinger emphasizes "AI website builder" and "AI content generation tools" extensively.

**The reality:** Their AI builder creates basic sites quickly. The AI writing tools generate generic content. Neither competes with Claude, ChatGPT, or dedicated AI tools.

**Impact level:** Low for users who have their own AI tools, Medium for users expecting AI-powered hosting to replace standalone tools

**Score:** 6/10 for AI specifically, 9/10 for actual hosting

---

### Con 6: Storage on Entry Plans ⭐⭐⭐

**The numbers:** Single plan = 50GB NVMe, Premium = 100GB NVMe

100GB is sufficient for most WordPress sites (which typically use 2-20GB). It's not enough for heavy video hosting or very large media libraries.

**Mitigation:** Store videos on YouTube/Vimeo and embed them. Use Cloudinary for large image libraries. Most sites never approach 100GB.

**Impact level:** Low for typical sites, Medium for media-heavy sites

**Score:** 7.5/10

**Can I use Hostinger for a professional business website?**

**Does Hostinger limit traffic on shared plans?**
Like all shared hosts, Hostinger's shared plans have resource limits. They don't publish specific traffic caps but manage server resources actively. Sites receiving 25,000-100,000 monthly visitors generally operate without issue. Very high-traffic sites may need to upgrade.
