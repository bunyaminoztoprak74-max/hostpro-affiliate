## 2. Hetzner — Best Compute Value

Hetzner's cloud workflow and per-core consistency make it a strong production choice. The trade-off is less storage per dollar and a location map that may not suit every audience. Read [Contabo vs Hetzner](/blog/contabo-vs-hetzner-2026).

## 3. DigitalOcean — Best for Developers

DigitalOcean costs more on raw specifications but offers excellent tutorials, APIs and managed services. Teams often recover the premium through faster deployment and less infrastructure assembly. See [Contabo vs DigitalOcean](/blog/contabo-vs-digitalocean-2026).

## 4–6. Vultr, Linode and OVHcloud

Vultr stands out for global regions and straightforward instances. Linode offers a mature developer platform backed by Akamai. OVHcloud is worth considering for network capacity and anti-DDoS protection, particularly in Europe. Compare backup, IPv4 and bandwidth charges at checkout.

## How to Choose

Start with the application's bottleneck. Databases and self-hosted collaboration tools often need RAM. Build jobs and busy APIs need consistent CPU. Media services need storage and transfer. Customer-facing sites need a region close to visitors and a tested recovery plan.

Budget for off-site backups, monitoring and administration. A $6 server that consumes hours of emergency work is not cheaper than a managed plan. Never launch before completing the steps in our [VPS setup guide](/blog/contabo-vps-setup-guide).

Contabo is the best headline value, Hetzner the best compute-first value and DigitalOcean the best developer experience. Choose based on workload rather than the largest specification table.
