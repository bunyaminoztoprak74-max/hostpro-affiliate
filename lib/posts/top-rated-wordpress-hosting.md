## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is WordPress.org's recommended host list trustworthy?**
Partially. Bluehost, SiteGround, DreamHost, and Hostinger are all legitimate hosting choices. The recommendation exists with financial components — hosts pay for featured placement. The list shouldn't be dismissed, but WP Engine and Kinsta (not listed) consistently outperform some listed partners in independent tests.

**What WordPress-specific features should I prioritize?**
For most WordPress sites: staging environment first (prevents broken live sites from updates), then quality caching (LiteSpeed Cache, SuperCacher, or EverCache), then daily backups (insurance against WordPress issues). WordPress expert support becomes important as your site grows.

**Can I run WordPress on a $2/month host?**
Technically yes — WordPress minimum requirements are modest. But the performance gap is significant. Namecheap at $1.98/month delivers 341ms TTFB. Hostinger at $2.99/month delivers 168ms TTFB. For $1/month more, you get 2x faster WordPress performance.

**How many WordPress sites can I host on one plan?**
Hostinger Premium: 100 WordPress sites. SiteGround GrowBig: unlimited. WP Engine Startup: 1 site. Kinsta Starter: 1 site. For agencies or developers managing many WordPress sites, Hostinger Premium or Cloudways provide the best multi-site economics.

**What's the best free WordPress hosting?**
WordPress.com offers free plans with limited features (subdomain, no plugins). For real WordPress with full control, there's no worthwhile free option. Budget $2.99-3.99/month for Hostinger or DreamHost as minimum spend for genuine self-hosted WordPress.
