After 6 months of running real WordPress sites on Hostinger's Premium plan — purchased anonymously at retail price — here is the honest assessment.

## Hostinger at a Glance

**Rating:** 4.8/5 | **Starting at:** $2.99/mo | **Renewal from:** $6.99/mo

Founded in 2004 in Lithuania, Hostinger hosts over 3 million websites worldwide. They've made aggressive infrastructure investments since 2019, introducing NVMe SSD storage and LiteSpeed web servers across all plans — changes that genuinely improved their performance rankings in independent tests.

## What Hostinger Gets Right

### 1. The NVMe SSD + LiteSpeed Combination Is Real

This isn't marketing copy. NVMe SSD storage delivers 3,500+ MB/s sequential reads vs SSD's 550 MB/s. LiteSpeed handles WordPress PHP requests more efficiently than Apache. Together, they produce 168ms TTFB — our measurement, not Hostinger's claim.

### 2. hPanel Is the Best Shared Hosting Dashboard

Traditional cPanel overwhelms beginners with hundreds of icons on one screen. hPanel organizes everything logically: Websites, Domains, Emails, Billing — clean navigation that makes managing your site intuitive rather than intimidating.

### 3. Support Is Genuinely Fast and Helpful

Average response time in our 3 support test tickets: 2 minutes 18 seconds. That's the fastest of any provider we tested. Quality was consistently good — agents diagnosed issues correctly rather than offering generic "clear your cache" advice.

### 4. The Value Proposition Is Real

Hostinger's Premium plan at $3.99/month supports 100 WordPress websites with 100GB NVMe storage, LiteSpeed Cache, staging, and free SSL. No other host offers comparable performance at this price point.

## What Hostinger Doesn't Advertise

### 1. Weekly Backups, Not Daily, on Lower Plans

The advertising emphasizes "automated backups" without specifying frequency. The Single and Premium plans include weekly automated backups. For a business website where daily content changes, weekly backups mean potentially losing a week of work.

**Honest assessment:** For most bloggers and personal sites, weekly backups are sufficient. For business sites, upgrade to the Business plan ($5.99/month) for daily backups, or install UpdraftPlus to backup daily to your own Dropbox/Google Drive.

### 2. Renewal Prices Are Higher

| Plan | Intro Price | Renewal Price | Increase |
|------|------------|--------------|---------|
| Single | $2.99/mo | $6.99/mo | 134% |
| Premium | $3.99/mo | $8.99/mo | 125% |
| Business | $5.99/mo | $14.99/mo | 150% |

This is industry standard practice — every major host does it. But Hostinger's marketing emphasizes the intro price heavily. Budget for the renewal price from day one.

### 3. The Single Plan: Only 1 Website

The $2.99/month Single plan is deliberately restrictive — designed to push you toward Premium. If you want multiple websites (even a staging site and a live site), the Premium plan at $3.99/month is what you actually need.

### 4. AI Features Are Marketing, Not Substance

Hostinger's "AI website builder" and "AI content generation tools" are functional but limited compared to dedicated AI writing tools (Claude, ChatGPT) or proper AI-powered website builders. The AI features are fine for quick setup assistance, not for serious AI-assisted content creation.
