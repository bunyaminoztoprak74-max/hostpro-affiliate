SEO gets WordPress sites free, sustainable traffic from Google. Unlike paid ads, organic traffic keeps coming even when you're not working. Here's the exact setup process.

## Why SEO Matters for WordPress Sites

A well-optimized WordPress blog can rank for hundreds of keywords and generate thousands of monthly visitors without any advertising spend. The key is getting the technical foundation right, then creating content that targets real search queries.

## The SEO Hierarchy

1. **Technical SEO** — your site must be crawlable and fast (covered by your hosting + setup)
2. **On-page SEO** — each post targets a specific keyword correctly
3. **Off-page SEO** — other sites link to yours (backlinks)

Most beginners focus too much on on-page and skip technical and backlinks. All three matter.

## Keyword Research for Beginners

You don't need paid tools to start. Free resources:

**Google Autocomplete:** Type your topic into Google and note the suggestions. These are real queries with real search volume.

**People Also Ask:** The expandable questions in Google results show common related queries — often easier to rank for.

**Ubersuggest (free):** Neil Patel's tool shows monthly search volume and keyword difficulty for free (limited searches).

**Focus on:** Keywords with under 30 keyword difficulty (KD) and 100+ monthly searches. These are rankable for new sites.

## Speed Is an SEO Factor

Google uses Core Web Vitals as a ranking signal. Your hosting directly impacts your score:
- **TTFB under 200ms:** Excellent (Hostinger, Cloudways, WP Engine)
- **TTFB 200–500ms:** Acceptable
- **TTFB over 500ms:** Hurts rankings

If you're on slow hosting, upgrading to [Hostinger](/review/hostinger) ($2.99/mo) improves both rankings and user experience.

## Content Strategy That Works

Write content that actually answers searcher intent:

- **Informational queries** ("how to start a blog") → Step-by-step guides
- **Comparison queries** ("hostinger vs bluehost") → Detailed comparisons with real data
- **Review queries** ("hostinger review 2026") → Honest reviews with pros/cons
- **Best-of queries** ("best web hosting 2026") → Listicles with methodology

## Schema Markup

Schema markup helps Google understand your content. Yoast handles basic article schema automatically. For additional schemas:
- **Review posts:** Add rating frontmatter (handled automatically on this site)
- **FAQ sections:** Use the FAQ block in Gutenberg
- **How-to guides:** Use Yoast's How-to block
