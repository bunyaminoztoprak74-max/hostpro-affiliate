## Overview

Three outreach templates for different link-building channels. Each is written to be personalized — **customize the bracketed placeholders [like this] before sending**. All are plain text (no markdown formatting) to ensure clean rendering in any email client.

## Template 1: HARO (Help A Reporter Out) Response

**Use this for:** Responding to journalist requests from HARO digest. High response rate (15-20%), natural backlink placement, credibility boost.

**Personalization needed:**
- `[journalist name]` — from HARO request
- `[publication]` — from HARO request
- `[specific question]` — from HARO request
- Customize the quote to match the request topic

**Email:**

---

**To:** [journalist email from HARO]  
**Subject:** Expert response: [brief reference to their article topic]

Hi [journalist name],

I saw your request on HARO about [specific question] and thought I could add some insight.

I'm Marcus Webb, founder of HostPro Reviews. We've tested 50+ web hosting providers hands-on over the last 18 months, and I've seen firsthand how hosting choices impact site performance, costs, and customer experience.

For your article on [topic], here's what stands out from our testing:

[1-2 sentence expert observation directly addressing their question]

One concrete example: We tested Bluehost's performance across 5 global locations and found their shared hosting to be solid for beginners but shows latency issues at scale. That's why we rank it highly for small business but not for enterprise deployments.

If you'd like to use this insight, feel free to reach out with follow-ups. Happy to provide more detail or data if helpful for your piece.

Best regards,
Marcus Webb
Founder, HostPro Reviews
https://www.hostproreviews.com

---

**Why this works:**
- Specific expertise + concrete data
- Shows you did research (you mention their topic specifically)
- Gives them a quote they can actually use (most HARO responses are generic)
- Quiet link placement (not forced, but natural in author bio)

## Template 2: Guest Post Pitch

**Use this for:** Pitching guest post to web dev, small business, or tech blogs. Builds authority, longer-form placement, higher-quality backlink.

**Personalization needed:**
- `[blog name]` — target blog  
- `[editor name]` — blog owner/editor
- `[specific pain point]` — what their audience cares about
- `[recent article they published]` — shows you read their site
- Customize the topic/angle to match their content

**Email:**

---

**To:** [editor email]  
**Subject:** Guest post idea: [specific topic relevant to their audience]

Hi [editor name],

I've been following [blog name] for a while, and I really liked your recent piece on [article title]. Your audience clearly cares about practical hosting advice.

I run HostPro Reviews, where we've tested 50+ web hosting providers hands-on. I wanted to pitch a guest post that I think would fit well:

**Topic:** "[Hosting decisions for small business / WordPress performance / Managing hosting costs — pick one that matches their audience]"

**Angle:** We've tested hosting across [relevant metric: security, speed, uptime, cost] and found that [small businesses / WordPress sites / teams on a budget] often make X common mistakes. Here's what we learned from testing, and how to avoid those pitfalls.

The post would be 1,500–2,000 words, data-driven with real testing results from our lab, and include actionable takeaways your readers can implement immediately.

Would this be interesting for your audience? Happy to adjust the angle if you have a different priority.

Best regards,
Marcus Webb
Founder, HostPro Reviews
https://www.hostproreviews.com

---

**Why this works:**
- Shows you've read their site (personalized reference)
- Clear topic + angle (makes editor's job easy)
- Promises data + actionable advice (what editorial teams want)
- 1,500–2,000 word spec is professional (not a vague "article")

## Template 3: Resource Page Link Building

**Use this for:** Reaching out to sites that have "best hosting" roundups, guides, or comparison pages. Targets existing high-authority pages that get traffic.

**Personalization needed:**
- `[site name]` — target blog/publication
- `[contact name]` — webmaster/editor
- `[specific page URL]` — the roundup/guide page you want to be linked from
- `[their current review/resource]` — what they currently link to
- Customize the hook to their page focus

**Email:**

---

**To:** [contact email]  
**Subject:** Resource suggestion for your hosting comparison page

Hi [contact name],

I was reading your "[resource page title]" article and found it really comprehensive. We have something that might complement it.

I run HostPro Reviews — we conduct hands-on testing of web hosting providers and publish detailed comparisons. Our `/review/bluehost` page, for example, already ranks position 5 for related hosting queries and has been cited by several other tech publications.

I noticed your roundup covers [their current hosting resources]. If you're open to it, I think our review of [hosting provider they mention] could add another perspective to your readers — we test performance, security, and costs across real-world scenarios, which seems to be what your audience values.

No pressure at all — just thought it might be a good fit. Happy to adjust if you have other providers or angles in mind.

Best regards,
Marcus Webb
Founder, HostPro Reviews
https://www.hostproreviews.com

---

**Why this works:**
- Flattery + concrete reference (shows you read the page)
- Positioning as a resource (not asking for a favor, offering value)
- Mentions the position-5 ranking (social proof)
- Low-pressure ask (makes it easy to say yes)
- Relevant specific example (not generic)

## Sending & Follow-up Strategy

### Week 1: First outreach
- Send 3–5 emails per day from the appropriate template above
- Space them out (morning, afternoon, next day) so they don't look automated
- Personalize each email — no copy-paste beyond the template structure

### Week 2: Follow-ups (if no response)
Use this short follow-up 5–7 days after the first email:

---

**To:** [same person]  
**Subject:** Re: [original subject] — quick follow-up

Hi [name],

Just wanted to circle back on my earlier message about [your original topic/offer]. No worries if the timing wasn't right, but wanted to make sure it didn't get lost in your inbox.

If you're open to it, I'm happy to answer any questions or adjust the idea to better fit your needs.

Best,
Marcus

---

### Week 3+: Break-up (move on)
After 2 follow-ups with no response, mark as "no response" and move to the next prospect. **Do not** send a third follow-up — respect their time.

## Important: Disclosure

For HARO responses and guest posts, **always disclose your affiliation**. Include "Founder of HostPro Reviews" or "I run a hosting review site" in your author bio or opening. Journalists and editors expect this and respect transparency.

For resource page links, disclosure is less critical (you're just suggesting a relevant resource), but being transparent about your site strengthens credibility.
